Tank & Pit

Maximum virtual energy per fill. Usually 100 unless event limits stint energy.
Baseline pit time: pit lane drive + stop + release, without any refuel or tires. Measure from pit-entry line to pit-exit line.
Seconds added to the stop for every 1% VE refueled. Time a known refuel amount in practice to calibrate.
Extra time when changing all four tires. Added on top of pit fixed + refuel.
Minimum total stop time when swapping drivers. If refuel+tires already take longer, swap is free.
Random variation in total stop time from crew timing. Typical 0.5–1s.

Tires

Total tires for the race. LMU events typically allocate 10–12 (quali + race combined). Subtract any used in quali. Each tire change uses 4 tires.
How much slower each lap becomes as tires age. 0.015 means lap 10 is 0.15s slower than lap 0.
Extra falloff near end of stint. 0.30 means 30% additional degradation at max stint length — the "cliff".
Reference stint length where the cliff is fully applied. Scales when the cliff starts biting.
Set-to-set variance in degradation. 0.15 = ±15% between tire sets — some go long, some fall off early.

Driver & Incidents

How consistent you are lap to lap. 0.3 = very consistent, 0.5 = typical, 0.8+ = inconsistent.
Chance per lap of a mistake (spin, off, minor contact). 0.002 = ~1 incident per 500 laps.
Typical time lost when an incident happens. Half of incidents cost more, half less.
How variable incident costs are. Higher = more extreme worst-cases (big offs, repairs).
Incident probability multiplier at full push. 1.3 = 30% more mistakes when pushing hard.

Traffic

Number of faster classes sharing the track. 0 = single class, 1 = Hypercar only, 2 = Hypercar + LMP2.
How many cars in the fastest class (usually Hypercar).
How many seconds per lap faster this class is than you. Drives blue flag frequency.
Cars in the second faster class (e.g. LMP2 at Le Mans). 0 if not running.
Pace delta to you from the second class.
Log-scale median of time lost per faster-car pass. μ=0 means median cost of 1 second.
Spread of pass costs. Higher = more variance between clean passes and bad ones.
Chance per lap of getting stuck behind a same-class car you can't easily pass.
Average laps it takes to get past once stuck. Real episodes can last longer.
Time lost per lap while stuck behind someone you can't pass.
Traffic multiplier in first 30% of race when field is bunched. 1.5 = 50% more encounters early.

Driver Swaps

Total drivers sharing the car. 2 is typical for 6h, 3+ for 12h/24h.
Longest a single driver can go before forced swap. WEC regs use ~80 min.
How much faster/slower each driver is vs the team average. 0.2s = small variance, 0.8s = one driver much quicker.

Number of pace levels tested between push and save. More = finer resolution, slower to run.
